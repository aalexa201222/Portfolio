I am proud to showcase a significant achievement from my high school years, where my team, CRAZY JUMP, represented our school in the esteemed Logipaignion 2017. The Logipaignion was an educational game contest organized by the University of Cyprus. Out of numerous participants, our team's entry, "how2internet," secured an impressive 2nd place. As a valued member of CRAZY JUMP, I played a pivotal role in the development of this educational game, particularly in coding and designing the level focused on cyberbullying.

As a key contributor to "how2internet," I assumed the crucial responsibility of coding and designing the level centered around cyberbullying. This level aimed to highlight the perils of online harassment and promote empathy and understanding among players. Through meticulous design and strategic gameplay, I created an interactive environment that effectively conveyed the gravity of this issue.

To ensure a captivating experience, our team dedicated considerable effort to the creation of original assets. We composed unique music tracks, designed captivating visuals, and incorporated immersive sound effects. Where necessary, we also utilized external online resources, always ensuring proper credit and adherence to copyright regulations.

"How2internet" features a player-driven progression system, allowing users to choose the level they wish to explore. Each level presents distinct challenges and dangers found in the online world. To progress, players must successfully navigate through these hazards until they encounter the final boss, a culmination of all the dangers they have faced. One of the standout features of our game is the integration of educational elements within the gameplay mechanics. The final boss not only poses a threat to the player's character but also presents thought-provoking questions about the dangers of the internet. This approach fosters a deeper understanding of online risks while engaging players in an exciting and interactive manner.
