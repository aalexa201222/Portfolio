Introduction

Virtual reality (VR) presents a transformative technology for underwater exploration, offering immersive experiences without the limitations and risks of physical scuba diving. This project proposes an innovative application of VR to explore underwater environments, focusing specifically on the ancient port of Amathus. By employing procedural generation techniques and artificial intelligence algorithms, the project aims to create a more immersive and realistic virtual underwater environment.

Procedural Generation of Underwater Vegetation

To enhance the immersion and realism of the virtual environment, the project utilizes procedural generation techniques. By dynamically generating diverse and lifelike underwater vegetation, the virtual environment replicates the complexity and richness of real-world ecosystems. This approach adds depth and visual appeal to the underwater exploration experience in the ancient port of Amathus.

Simulating Fish Movements with Artificial Intelligence

In addition to procedural generation, the project incorporates artificial intelligence algorithms to simulate fish movements within the virtual environment. By leveraging AI-driven fish behavior, the virtual underwater world becomes even more realistic and believable. Users will feel as though they are truly diving in the ancient harbor, observing fish in their natural habitats, and further enhancing the overall immersion.

Simulated Dive through Amathus Harbor

The main outcome of this research project is a simulated dive through the ancient port of Amathus. This immersive experience allows users to explore the underwater world without the need for physical scuba diving. Through the power of VR technology, individuals can embark on a virtual journey, discovering the beauty and wonders of the underwater environment within the historic harbor.

Contributing to VR Applications for Underwater Exploration

By exploring the potential of virtual reality in the uncharted domain of underwater environments, this research project contributes to the development of new applications and experiences. The aim is to expand our understanding and appreciation of underwater worlds, while providing safe and accessible means of exploration. The project offers a stepping stone for future advancements in VR-based underwater exploration, pushing the boundaries of technology and expanding the possibilities within this field.
